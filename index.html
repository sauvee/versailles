• Welcome to Versailles!

    Versailles Castle is the 5th most visited place in France.
    It is also one of the symbols of French monarchy.
    Let's visit it together!
    (and if you'd like to have some music for the atmosphere, click on the player under the picture!)

  • Where?

    The city of Versailles is only 10 miles southwest of Paris.

    It became the center of political power from 1682 to 1789, under Louis XIV's rule.

  • Louis XIII

    (1601-1643)

    Louis XIII, also known as "Louis the Just", decided to make of Versailles a hunting lodge in 1624. His reign is marked by his fight against the Habsburg Empire.

    Louis XIV

    (1638-1715)

    Louis XIV, also called "Louis the Great" or "The Sun King" ruled France for 72 years. He created a centralized state in Versailles and an absolute monarchy.

    Louis XV

    (1710-1774)

    Louis XV is also known as "Louis the Well Beloved". Scholars believe he actually discredited his great grand father Louis XIV's absolute monarchy

    Louis XVI

    (1754-1793)

    Louis XVI attempted to reform France, but ultimately, the French Revolution happened and he and his wife Marie-Antoinette were executed by guillotine

    Who's who? These four kings either built or lived in Versailles. Click on their portraits to find out more about them.

  • Who built what?

  • The Hall of Mirrors

    The Hall of Mirrors was achieved by architect Jules Hardouin-Mansart in 1684. There are 17 mirror-clad arches, reflecting 17 arcaded windows from which you can see the gardens. In total no less than 357 mirrors were used in the room.

  • The chapel

    The Chapel was the last major building of Louis XIV. The present chapel is actually the fifth in the history of the castle and was finished in 1710. Louis XIV attended the service every day.

  • The gardens

    In 1661, Louis XIV ordered André Le Nôtre (1613-1700) plans for Versailles' gardens. The works took forty years to complete. 300 years later, more than six million visitors walk in the gardens every year.

    In 2013, Versailles celebrated Le Nôtre's 400th anniversary with a new exposition.
